Blogging for Business

Jun 17th, 2003 | Comments

Every once in a while, I often think about how it would be nice to start my own business. Maybe get into building small-to-medium scale business websites for the locals. With all the great open source software out there these days, it’s never been easier to dive into building a business on the web. Seeing how various people have used their blogs for business is interesting. It’s a great way to promote yourself and your qualifications. It’s much better than throwing your resume on a website and emailing to people. With a blog, you can post work that you’ve done, works in progress, and various business related things that can give a client prospect a better idea of your qualifications. Adam Kalsey just wrote an interesting blog about building your brand and how blogs could improve your business image by treating your blog as a marketing vehicle. Since I put up this blog, I’ve originally thought about making this a personal blog/website. However, I find that most of the time, the blogs I read are mostly related to what I do in my professional life… like technology topics that interest me. However, since I’m trying to avoid creating multiple blogs, I’ve also been posting things that may not be related to my professional life. Now, I’m in a pickle… do I want to keep doing that or do I want this site to be more of a blog about a certain set of topics related to my professional life. Certainly, my readership might go up since I don’t think there are very many people out there that want to read about my dog Truman. Hmmm… gotta decide what to do with this site soon.

When the Elephants Dance

Jun 15th, 2003 | Comments

Just found out from Lindsay, Cindy’s cousin, that she’s going to the Philippines for a Peace Corps assignment. She’s not sure where she’s going yet, but it sure does sound exciting. Although there’s been a lot of news about terrorist groups in the Philippines, from what my parents tell me (they go back every year) the terrorist action that the Abu Sayyaf Group has been involved in has been isolated to the southern islands. Anyway, this book, When the Elephants Dance, is a book written by Tess Uriza Holthe who is a Filipino American writer… one of very few. I personally have not read this book yet, but my wife and her mother have and both have truly enjoyed reading it to learn more about the Filipino culture. The story is about life in the World War II Philippines when the Japanese invaded and took over the country. It’s the next book on my long list of books to read… I’m one of those people who like to buy books and read it a year later.

Geocaching

Jun 14th, 2003 | Comments

A few months ago, we heard this article on NPR about the growing popularity of a new adventure game called“geocaching.” After that, Cindy’s co-worker, Sandi, told us about it. She apparently has been “geocaching” for a few years when the adventure game started out. The game is similar to that of a scavenger hunt. The difference is, this is done with the aid of a GPS device. We thought it sounded interesting and so we borrowed a GPS receiver from Sandi for a weekend and decided to go look for our first geocache – conveniently located near us at Redwood Regional Park. We had a great time, after completely missing a turn and hiking around an extra mile or two, we found our first geocache and logged our find. It’s a fun game and a great use for a GPS receiver. More than anything, this adventure game is an excuse to get out and go hiking and discover new places you wouldn’t otherwise go to. It gives you an objective for your hike. We’re hooked… now we just need to find time amongst all the other things we like to do on weekends.

Garmin Rhino 120

Jun 14th, 2003 | Comments

After doing a lot of research on what GPSr to get for geocaching, we ended up with a Garmin Rhino 120. It’s a great little GPSr that combines a map based GPS with an FRS (Family Radio Service, a walkie-talkie like CB radio service) and GMRS (similar to FRS but longer range). So far it’s been a great little GPS device. My only complaint is that it doesn’t work very well under dense tree cover, but that seems to be the weakness behind Garmin’s GPS receivers. Magellan’s receivers are apparently better under tree cover.

Mozilla Firebird… a Better Browser?

Jun 13th, 2003 | Comments

Looking for a better browser? Tired of Internet Explorer? I ran across this post a few days ago and decided to try out Mozilla’s new browser, Firebird. So far it’s been a nice experience. It’s fast, stable, and amazingly, a lot of pages I thought would have thrown up work in Firebird. On top of all this, it’s a small footprint on your machine. Because it’s Mozilla, the engine behind Netscape Navigator, the http headers sent out make it seem like a Netscape browser. So, a lot of websites coded specifically for Netscape work well in this browser (not that that means much). Give it a whirl. The install is very unobtrusive… it’s basically packaged as a zip file (for win32)…so it doesn’t take over your machine. Ben Gooder, another blogger who contributes to the Firebird project, has put together a list of reasons why you should switch over to Firebird. By the way, for you PeopleSoft heads out there – PeopleSoft runs great on this browser… of course.
